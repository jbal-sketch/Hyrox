Key Nutrition Principles

1. Protein Timing

  • 25-30g protein within 30min post-workout
  • 30-40g protein at each main meal
  • Bedtime protein (casein or Greek yogurt) to support recovery

2. Carb Timing

  • Load carbs around training (before/after)
  • Lower carbs on rest days
  • Never train fasted for high-intensity sessions

3. Hydration

  • 2.5-3L water daily
  • Electrolytes during gym sessions (especially with wall balls/burpees)
  • Increase on long run days

Supplements

Essential

  • Whey Protein: 1-2 scoops daily (post-workout or to hit protein targets)
  • Creatine Monohydrate: 5g daily (improves power output for sleds/wall balls)
  • Electrolytes: During long sessions (2+ hours)

Optional but Beneficial

  • Omega-3 (Fish Oil): 2-3g daily (recovery, inflammation)
  • Vitamin D: 2000-4000 IU daily (especially in UK winter)
  • Magnesium: 300mg before bed (sleep, recovery, muscle function)
  • Caffeine: 200mg pre-race (proven performance enhancer)

Race Week Nutrition (Week 11)

  • Monday-Wednesday: Maintain normal eating
  • Thursday: Start increasing carbs slightly (extra rice/pasta)
  • Friday: High carb day (350g carbs) - fuel up
  • Saturday: Moderate carbs, familiar foods, early dinner
  • Sunday Race Day: Light breakfast 2-3hrs before (porridge, banana, toast with honey)

Weekly Check-ins

Track Weekly

  • Weight (Monday morning, same time, after bathroom)
  • Body measurements (waist, hips)
  • Training performance (are you hitting targets?)
  • Energy levels, sleep quality, recovery

Adjust If

  • Losing more than 0.4kg/week → Add 100-150 calories
  • Not losing weight for 2 weeks → Reduce 100-150 calories
  • Performance dropping → Increase carbs around workouts
  • Feeling exhausted → Increase overall calories or rest more
